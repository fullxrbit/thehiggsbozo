### A review of Project Hail Mary
Project Hail Mary is a hard sci-fi film about space, personal development, bravery, struggle, alien life, science, friendship and love. That summarizes my experience at the cinema pretty well. Before you continue reading further, may I warn you that this review contains a heavy dose of spoilers.

The movie succeeds in presenting a funny, clumsy version of Grace (the main character) in a world on the verge of civilizational collapse. Grace is portrayed by Ryan Gosling, who is pretty much the only actor in the bulk of the movie, except for some parts. Nonetheless, this allows for a better exploration of the character and his relationship with Rocky. The fact that the better part of the story happens just between Rocky and Grace means that the viewer is allowed into a deep sense of intimacy between the two characters. 

Another positive aspect is Grace's character development throughout the film. At the beginning of the film he exhibits asocial behavior. He often appeals to humour as a defense mechanism, is disengaged from academia due to rejection and avoids any meaningful relationships. Nonetheless, as the movie progresses, we see him move away from these tendencies and more towards an open character, mostly because of Rocky. Perhaps because he sees in him what he lacks, which is courage and honesty. 

Ultimately, Project Hail Mary was a great experience which I'd recommend to anyone interested in science fiction and would like to spend their afternoon watching a story about friendship.
